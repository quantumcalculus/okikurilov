I am a computing evangelist and a skeptical optimist, passionate about supporting and building moonshot projects in Deeptech and Biotech. Human-centered applications are my primary focus, and I co-aproach this through a wide involvement in NGO and non-profits focused on civil rights advocacy.
Currently, I'm pursuing an MPhil in Physics at Trinity Hall, University of Cambridge, under the supervision of Dr Diana Fusco. I build HPC-compatible LAMMPS/GROMACS packages to investigate macroscopic mechanical properties that emerge from the active-passive intersurface interasctions in biomaterials and implement stochastical methods to foresee the behaviour under extreme physical conditions.
On the industry side, I'm was an Enterpreneur-in-Residence at Founders @ Cambridge, part of Cambridge Enterprise. I was also a scout for Built in LA and spent 2 years in a Deeptech-focused investment role at the SFO. I serve as a technical advisor to 2 London-based pre-seed startups.
I hold a BSc in CompBio + Mathematical Modelling from UCLA. My undergraduate thesis advisor is Dr Jun Chen and I'm affiliated with Soft Matter Research Group. I've also worked at PARISlab and Elegant Mind as ML & Data Sci research assistant.
My research interests span soft magnetic materials, biomaterials, AI-assisted materials characterization, non-equilibrium dynamics of quantum materials and quantum information theory. With the power of computation, our possibilities are limitless. Check out Microsoft Azure Quantum blog!
